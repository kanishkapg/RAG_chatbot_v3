Corporate Governance and Roles & Responsibilities of Stakeholders

Sri Lanka Telecom PLC is a public listed company and is required to comply with the provisions of the Companies Act No. 07 of 2007, the Articles of Association (“AOA”) of the company and the CSE listing rules. Apart from statutory and regulatory requirements, SLT is required to establish best practices for corporate governance as detailed in the Code of Practice on Corporate Governance 2017 (“CG Code”), to ensure that they are serving the needs of the shareholders, employees, management, customers and other stakeholders effectively and responsibly.

The Board of Directors have adopted a resolution detailing norm of corporate governance and related roles and responsibilities of stakeholders. Accordingly, the essential information of the resolution is detailed below for your attention, and you are hereby advised to comply with the same with immediate effect.

- That all Directors discharge their duties and responsibilities as Non-Executive Directors (“NEDs”).
- As per the Articles of Association of SLT and guidelines provided in the Board Procedure Manual wherein it clearly requires demarcation of the responsibilities of the Chief Executive Officer (“CEO”) and the Chairman of the Board establishing good corporate governance practices.
- That none of the NEDs chair any management meeting other than duly appointed Board Sub-Committees with a formal term of reference by the Board of Directors where the Board Sub-Committees are strengthened to fulfil regulatory requirements and to exercise better control over business operations and to provide their observations and recommendations in reports to the Board on a regular basis.
- That the CEO is given the authority and held responsible for the daily management of the business and provides leadership to the executive team for executing the Company’s strategic and operating plans.
- That none of the NEDs can individually issue instructions to management without a clear Board mandate or by the Board Sub-Committees who are empowered to issue such mandates.
- That none of the NEDs are authorised to work for the Company without prior board approval other than a director who acts by himself or by his firm in a professional capacity for the Company and he or his firm shall be entitled to remuneration for professional services as if he were not a Director.
- That none of the NEDs are authorised to make media or other public statements without prior Board approval, recognising that these statements will be taken as statements of the Board of Directors.
- That all NEDs will be only allowed, other than Directors’ fees, costs that are incurred wholly and necessarily in relation to their role as a Director of SLT, and that SLT will provide support services to NEDs only to carry out their role as NEDs.

In addition to the above, please be advised that the management is not supposed to share confidential information such as market intelligence, sensitive business information etc. with NEDs.
