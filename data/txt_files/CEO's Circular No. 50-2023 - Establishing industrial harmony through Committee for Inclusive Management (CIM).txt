CEO’s Office, Sri Lanka Telecom PLC, Colombo 01.

To All Staff,

Establishing industrial harmony through Committee for Inclusive Management (CIM)

With a view of improving the employee – employer relationship within SLT, the “Committee for Inclusive Management (CIM)” concept is introduced. Trade Unions can easily raise their issues/concerns and share their ideas and proposals on company growth initiatives etc. to a single point of contact so that the same can be followed up and receive the responses from the correct stakeholder at the said forum. Similarly, management also can make their suggestions.

All trade unions are hereby invited to follow new procedure in communicating with management on the matters pertaining to respective members and the trade union as a whole as rolled out in the presentation to all Trade Union representatives on 10/07/2023 at the SLT Auditorium..

1. CIM
   CIM shall consist of following members/ parties
   1.1 President or Vice President and Secretary or Deputy Secretary of each Trade Union (must be in current employment of SLT) will represent the matters pertaining to their respective membership category.
   1.2 All Chief Officers
   1.3 Chairman of CIM (a GCXO/CXO to be appointed on rotation by the CEO)
   1.4 GM/HR & IR
   1.5 DGM/ER & IR - (Secretary to the CIM)
   1.6 Manager ER & IR - (CIM Coordinator)

2. Pre-CIM
   There shall be a meeting prior to the CIM for the management team. It is consisted of
   2.1 CEO and all Chief Officers
   2.2 Chairman of CIM (a GCXO to /CXO be appointed on rotation by the CEO)
   2.3 GM/HR & IR
   2.4 DGM/ER & IR - (Secretary to the CIM)
   2.5 MER & IR - (CIM Coordinator)

3. Functions of Committee for Inclusive Management

CIM should function as the point of contact for the trade unions to raise the grievances of employees / issues to be sorted out by line managers / other concerns / suggestions / proposals / feedback, etc. of Trade Unions, to the attention of management of SLT and receive feedback of management in an effective and efficient manner.

Table 01. Responsibility & Timelines of CIM

| Sr No | Action    | Responsibility    | Timeline    |
|---|---|---|---|
| 01    | Submit the CIM discussion points using the provided format -Annexure 01    | President or Secretary of Trade Unions    | By the first Wednesday of each month to CIM Coordinator    |
| 02    | Distribute within 24 hours, as relevant, the CIM discussion points to respective CXOs.    | GM/HR & IR (Supported by CIM Coordinator)    | By the \( 01^st \) Thursday of each month (day following the \( 01^st \) Wednesday)    |
| 03    | Conduct the pre-CIM meeting to take actions or prepare responses with regard to the submitted discussion points of the trade unions.    | GM/HR & IR (Supported by the CIM Secretary)    | 1.30 p.m. on \( 02^{nd} \) Wednesday of each month    |
| 04    | Conduct the CIM meeting in a reasonable and cordial manner    | CIM Chairman    | 1.30 p.m. on 03rd Wednesday of each month    |

(If a holiday falls on the Wednesday Pre – CIM & CIM will be held on the day before).

Relevant background information will also be used to justify the position taken by the relevant chief officer at this forum. Any issue which cannot be decided by the CXO in his capacity, should be discussed with the CEO or relevant parties for arriving at the most suitable response.

CXO’s participation is mandatory for Pre-CIM & CIM. However, due to unavoidable circumstances a GM should be appointed by the respective Chief Officer if he/she is unable to participate in the Pre-CIM or CIM and such representative officer should be well prepared to represent the CXO.

4. Ground rules for TU and Management

4.1 Effective management of time at CIM meetings (consume an average of 2 hours per meeting)
4.2 Maintain cordiality and be reasonable in communication
4.3 Mutual Respect for all the parties
4.4 Trade unions must submit clear and precise discussion points (briefly)

Management should take the steps to attend the concerns of the Trade Unions where possible without waiting for the Pre-CIM or CIM meetings and such resolved issues and actions taken should be clarified by the relevant CXO to the trade unions at the CIM Meeting.

Exceptional issues or matters which require immediate attention /action by the management will have to be referred to the relevant CXO by DGM/ER & IR and relevant actions / responses should be communicated to trade unions prior to CIM wherever possible.

Management can share the issues for which they want the support of trade unions to sort them out and invite them to extend their support to resolving such issues at the CIM Meeting.

The records of all the concerns of Trade Unions submitted and responses of management including the actions taken, should be well documented and maintained by the CIM Coordinator.

All employees, whether members or non-members of trade unions, will express their work-related concerns and issues to their respective supervisor and thereafter follow line protocol. Only the issues/concerns that have not been successfully addressed by line management, could be taken up at the CIM.

The fullest support from trade unions are requested to adhere to the new procedure, which will ensure an accurate and quick responses for their concerns by the management.

This Circular will be effective from 2nd of August 2023.

Janaka R. Abeysinghe
Chief Executive Officer
