Introduction of the Terms of Reference of Brand Council

The Brand Council of SLT-MOBITEL serves as a platform for strategic discussions and decision-making regarding brand management, including branding and brand communication. Comprised of key stakeholders from various functions, the council’s primary objective is to ensure effective brand stewardship and alignment with the organization’s vision.

The council is composed of the CEO-SLT as the Chairperson and GCMO as the Secretary. Members include the COO-Mobitel, the CEOs of the subsidiaries and other related GMM, DGMM of SLT-MOBITEL. Additionally, representatives of members, other functional Chief Officers or their designated representatives and required 3rd parties will be invited as required.

Responsibilities of the Brand Council include overseeing brand strategy implementation, reviewing and approving major initiatives and campaigns, assessing project alignment with brand identity, and promoting a culture of brand stewardship throughout the organization. New brand logos and periodic brand performance updates are also presented and discussed in the council.

The council holds bi-monthly meetings to discuss upcoming campaigns, brand strategy, performance, and challenges. External consultants may be engaged to provide expertise on specific brand-related topics.

These Terms of Reference provide a framework for effective brand management and decision-making, subject to periodic reviews to ensure alignment with organizational goals and market dynamics.

# TERMS OF REFERENCE: BRAND COUNCIL

1. Purpose:

The Brand Council of SLT-MOBITEL creates a ground to strategic discussions and decision-making related to effective brand management including branding, brand communication & etc. and its execution with cross functional teams. The council's primary objective is to ensure effective brand stewardship and alignment with the organization's vision, while involving key stakeholders from various functions.

4. Responsibilities:

- **Oversee the implementation of the brand strategy** and drive brand guideline aligning throughout the organization with the organization's vision, values, people strategies, brand guides, and brand rollout initiatives.

- **Monitor and Review brand portfolio and the hierarchy** of the group.

- **Foster brand consistency** across all touchpoints and brand communications.

- **Monitor and evaluate the brand's performance, perception and market trends.**

- **To review, approve and ratify** brand-related major initiatives, campaigns, and partnerships.

- **Assess proposed projects,** initiatives, or partnerships to ensure their alignment with the brand identity, positioning, and strategic objectives.

- **Directions on product, services & customer experiences** initiatives which impact on brand consistency and relevance.

- **Promote a culture of brand stewardship,** ensuring that the brand is integrated into all aspects of the organization's activities.

- **New Brand Logos** of the SLT-MOBITEL group, including subsidiaries to be presented and get approved in the Brand Council.

- **Provide periodic updates** to the relevant stakeholders on brand performance, initiatives, and challenges.

5. Meetings and Operations:

- The Brand Council is scheduled bi-monthly, specifically on the first and third weeks of each month. Any modifications to this meeting frequency or schedule will be made based on directives from the Chairperson.

- Meetings may include presentations, reports, and discussions on upcoming campaigns, brand strategy, initiatives, performance, challenges and etc.

- SLT and its all subsidiaries need to present new brand initiatives, upcoming campaigns concerns to the Brand Council.

- Materials of the Brand Council pack should be submitted to the council secretary at least 3 days prior to the scheduled meeting date. The secretary will then share the Brand Council Pack to the members.

- The council shall maintain minutes of the meetings, documenting key decisions, action points, and responsibilities assigned to individuals or teams. Meeting minutes should be signed by the Chairperson and Secretary to share with the council

Abstract of the terms.

1. Brand strategy:
   - Brand strategy refers to the long-term plan and direction for building and managing the brand, including its positioning, target audience, competitive differentiation, and messaging.

2. Brand guideline:
   - Brand guidelines are a set of rules and standards that outline how the brand should be portrayed across various channels and touchpoints. They provide instructions on logo usage, typography, colors, tone of voice, imagery, and other visual and verbal elements.

3. Brand portfolio:
   - Brand portfolio refers to the collection of brands owned and managed by a company or organization. It includes all the individual brands, sub-brands, and product lines that exist under the company's umbrella. Managing a brand portfolio involves strategic decisions regarding brand architecture, brand extensions, and brand hierarchy.

4. Customer touchpoints:
   - Customer touchpoints are the various interactions and points of contact between a customer and the brand throughout their journey. These can include physical locations, websites, social media platforms, customer service interactions, advertising, packaging, and any other channels where customers engage with the brand. Managing customer touchpoints involves ensuring a consistent and positive brand experience at each stage of the customer journey.

5. Brand consistency:
   - Brand consistency refers to the unified and coherent presentation of the brand across all touchpoints and communications. It ensures that the brand's visual identity, messaging, values, and customer experience remain consistent and recognizable, regardless of the channel or medium.

6. Brand stewardship:
   - Brand stewardship involves the responsible and strategic management of a brand. It includes overseeing and protecting the brand's identity, reputation, and equity. Brand stewards ensure that the brand is consistently represented, aligned with the organization's vision, and effectively communicated to stakeholders.

7. External consultants:
   - External consultants are experts or specialists hired from outside the organization to provide additional insights, knowledge, or support on specific brand-related topics. They may be engaged to offer guidance on regulatory issues, technology trends, digital media strategies, or facilitate discussions on sensitive matters.
