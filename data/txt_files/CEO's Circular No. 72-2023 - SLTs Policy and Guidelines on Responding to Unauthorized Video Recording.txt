CEO’s Office, Sri Lanka Telecom PLC, Colombo 01
5th October 2023

To all employees of SLT,

SLT’s policy and guidelines on responding to unauthorized Video Recording/Photographing of SLT Premises and/or Employee activities.

This circular describes the Sri Lanka Telecom PLC’s policy on video recording of its premises or employee activities by external parties without obtaining proper permissions and provides guidelines on handling such incidents wisely and lawfully, as responsible officers/employees of SLT.

Sri Lanka Telecom’s policy on Unauthorized Video Recording/Photographing of its Premises and/or Employees.

- SLT premises are considered private property, and the owner (SLT management) has the right to decide whether employees and outsiders are allowed to take video recording/photographing there or not. In addition, SLT has the responsibility to ensure that its properties, employees, and customer sensitive information are not recorded unnecessarily and shared with third parties without any knowledge of the SLT Management. Further, it is our duty to ensure the security of our customers and employees, inside the SLT premises.
  )
- Based on the above facts, SLT’s policy is that no external personnel (including SLT-Mobitel customers) or internal employees shall be allowed to video record/photograph the premises owned by or legitimately used by Sri Lanka Telecom or its subsidiaries without written permission from Chief Administrative Officer (CAO) of Sri Lanka Telecom Plc.
  Such permission will be given by CAO only upon a justifiable reason(s) and in consultation with necessary security and legal officers/authorities.

- SLT will take the utmost care and take every possible precautionary measure to avoid such unauthorized video recording/photographing and should refrain from antagonizing or provoking external parties for such action.

- In any case, if such an incident is taking place (or has happened), SLT and its employees shall act very responsibly to manage the incident, remove the personnel from SLT premises undisruptive manner, report it to the respective authorities immediately and take necessary countermeasures within the established legal and regulatory framework, preventing any harm to the good image of the company and its employees.

- In the process of preventing, avoiding or ceasing such incidents, SLT and its employees shall take extreme care to avoid any harm, mentally or physically, to any of the parties involved.

Guidelines on handling of the incidents that violates the above policy.

- If any video recording/photographing incident connected to SLT Group interest, is noticed inside or outside of SLT-Mobitel Premises, SLT Employees or Security officers (SLT owned or Private Security) who noticed the incident shall politely request the person/s involved to produce the document of permission from SLT’s authorized officer.
- If they fail to submit such a document, immediately make the superiors (and the RTOM in case of a Regional Office) aware and respectfully inform them that video recording/photographing is not permitted inside the premises and inform them to obtain permission from CAO with proper justification.
- It is the responsibility of SLT Owned or Private security staff on duty at the premises to record the incident in their official logbook, inform the Manager/Security and follow the given security guidelines.
- In case they do not favorably respond to the request, inform the superiors and the Manager Security of SLT. Together with the superior officer (or RTOM), make another effort to explain politely and prevent the incident.
- If the external party or the employee involved still continues the unauthorized videoing/photographing even after the advice of Superiors/Manager, the Manager, Senior officer in-charge must call the security staff to assist them in stopping the incident and

removing the persons from the premises but should take care not to apply force on them or make any verbal or physical harassment.

- Take utmost care,
  - Not to engage in arguments or confrontations with such person/s
  - Not to take any actions that may harm the involved person/s mentally or physically.
  - Not to violate any civil laws or established organizational regulations.
  - Not to involve in any actions that would impair the good image of SLT-Mobitel.

- All employees are strictly advised to avoid any actions that may lead to confrontations with such persons. In the event such person(s) are mentally or physically abused, the company is unable to lawfully restrict the victim or a companion of the victim from video recording/photographing.

- If any employee engages in such confrontation with a customer or any external party while at work violating this policy and guidelines shall be subjected to the disciplinary procedure of the organization. Further, SLT may not be able to support them in case of any legal procedures against them under the civil/criminal laws.

- If the security staff is unable to manage the situation, the security Officer In-charge of the premises shall call the police and get their support to remove the offenders upon obtaining permission from the Manager Security of SLT.

- RTO Manager/ Senior Manager In-Charge shall follow the subsequent security and legal proceedings together with Manager/ Security of SLT, as guided by the CAO.

- In carrying out the above-mentioned tasks/duties by SLT employees, SLT will take the necessary actions to safeguard the security of SLT employees and their belongings within the legal framework of the country.
